### 2. Necchi Lydia 3 (Type 544)
[website]()

Videos: [How to use](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bwMmUhC9rMk) | [Manual](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yK48Jn7cgqg)

* **The Machine:** The **Lydia 3 (Type 544)** is an Italian-designed, highly stylish free-arm sewing machine from the 1970s. It features a distinctive lever-activated reverse stitch on the front (unlike the push-button on older Lydias).
* **Notice:** While beautiful and smooth, this model family is famous among vintage mechanics for its **internal thermoplastic cam stack, which is highly prone to splitting/cracking over time**. When this cracks, the machine's decorative and zig-zag stitches fail, though it can often still sew a solid straight stitch.
